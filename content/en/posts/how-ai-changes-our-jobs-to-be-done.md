### Jobs to be done

There are three key approaches to innovation and product development:

- Assuming or guessing what customers want and validating with them afterwards.
- Using surveys and interviews to ask customers what they want; "voice of the customer".
- Closely observing customer behaviour in order to identify for what "jobs" do people "hire" specific tools and services, even when customers have no conscious notion of that.

Guessing is the easiest, but "jobs to be done" gives the best information and is widely considered the way to "take luck out of innovation processes".
